Our Story

2022

How We Met

We first met in 2022. Our first meeting wasn't great. Cathy had just come back from the Poland-Ukraine border, where she'd been helping people from eastern Ukraine who were forced to leave their homes because of the war. She said something in Russian to Oleks, and he was already overwhelmed by everything going on and didn't know how to react. Not exactly the best start for either of us.

Big Sky

2023

A New Beginning

Fast forward to September 2023. We took a trip to Seattle with friends — or, as a few in the group liked to say, “You crashed our girls’ trip.” That’s when things started to shift. After that trip, we began spending a lot more time together. Our relationship had a slow start, but it didn’t take long for us to grow close.

Seattle adventure that changed everything

2024

Adventures Together

By 2024, we had found our rhythm and fully embraced the things we love most: skiing, hiking, random adventures, and getting outside whenever we could. Nature became more than just a playground; it became the backdrop to many of our favorite memories.

Finding our rhythm in the mountains

2025

The Proposal

During our backpacking trip on the Continental Divide Trail, Oleksandr proposed at 12,500 feet on a mountain pass at the start of Colorado, symbolizing a new beginning as we entered a new state. He had prepared an entire speech but got nervous and forgot it, so all he managed to say was, “You know I love you very much… will you marry me?” Catherine responded with, “OMG, yes I will!”

12,500 feet - She said yes!

2026

Our Wedding Day

Now, we can't wait to gather with our family and friends in Montana to celebrate the next chapter of our lives. The mountains have been part of our story from the very beginning, so it feels only fitting to begin our married life surrounded by them.

Getting to Bozeman

The venue is about 25 minutes from downtown Bozeman. We recommend arriving a day early to explore the beautiful surroundings and acclimate to the mountain altitude. The closest airport is Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport (BZN).

Montana Formal

Dress to celebrate under the mountain sky!

What is Montana Formal?

Think elegant with a relaxed mountain vibe! We want you looking great AND feeling comfortable as we dance the night away under the stars. Picture cocktail attire that can handle a grassy venue and mountain breezes.

Bottom line: Dress up and look festive, but keep it comfy! We're on grass under the open sky, so choose shoes that won't sink and bring layers for when the sun sets. Most importantly — wear something you can celebrate in!

Vyshyvanka

Celebrate with Ukrainian embroidered tradition!

What is a Vyshyvanka?

A vyshyvanka (вишиванка) is a beautiful Ukrainian embroidered shirt or blouse! Each piece tells a story through intricate patterns and colors that vary by region. Many are treasured family heirlooms passed down through generations.

The Beautiful Tradition

  • Hand-embroidered with gorgeous geometric or floral patterns
  • Each region of Ukraine has its own unique style and colors
  • Made from comfortable linen or cotton
  • Can be dressed up or down depending on the style
  • Worn with pride at celebrations and special occasions

How to Style It for the Wedding

  • Men: Pair with nice dress pants or dark slacks — Franko Style!
  • Women: Wear as a dress or blouse with skirt/dress pants
  • Layer under a blazer for a formal touch, or rock it solo
  • Perfect way to blend cultural heritage with wedding elegance

We'd love to see you in one! If you have Ukrainian heritage or own a vyshyvanka, we'd be absolutely thrilled to see you wear it! It's such a meaningful way to honor Ukraine and celebrate culture. Don't have one? No worries at all — Montana Formal is just as perfect!

While You're Here

A few of our favorite spots around Bozeman — feel free to explore.

Dinner

  • Little Star Diner — chef-driven small plates, quietly one of the best in town
  • Copper Whiskey Bar & Grill — whiskey, steaks & cocktails downtown
  • Whistle Pig Korean — Korean BBQ — our first date was here 💕
  • Dave's Sushi — if you want to die (here's why)
  • Plaza Azteca — casual Mexican, big portions

Breakfast & Coffee

  • Mr. Burritos — burritos done right — Oleks' favorite
  • Aurora French Bakery — croissants, pastries & a quick coffee
  • Studio Coffee — Cathy's favorite ☕
  • Wild Crumb — bakery + breakfast sandwiches, locals' go-to
  • Western Cafe — old-school Montana diner, full of character
  • Treeline Coffee Roasters — solid coffee for before the day starts

Hiking

  • Peets Hill — short walk-up with town views, perfect morning loop
  • "M" Trail — quick climb to Bozeman's iconic lookout (~30 min up)
  • Drinking Horse Mountain — easy switchbacks, 360° views from the top
  • Sypes Canyon — moderate canyon hike just north of town
  • Middle Cottonwood — forested Bridger canyon, creek the whole way up
  • North Cottonwood — quieter neighbor to Middle Cottonwood, longer & bigger views
  • Palisade Falls — short paved walk to a waterfall in Hyalite Canyon
  • Lava Lake — moderate ~6 mi round-trip to an alpine lake
